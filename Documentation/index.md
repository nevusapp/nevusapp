# Nevus - User Guide

**Welcome to Nevus!** 🎯

Nevus is your personal mole tracking companion that helps you monitor skin changes over time. This guide will help you get started and make the most of the app's features.

> ⚠️ **Medical Disclaimer**: Nevus is a tracking tool, not a medical device. It is not intended for diagnosis or treatment. Always consult a healthcare professional for medical advice and regular skin examinations.

---

## 📱 Quick Start

### Getting Started in 3 Steps

1. **Add Your First Mole**
   - Tap the "+" button
   - Select body region and side
   - Take a photo with your camera
   - Add optional notes

2. **Track Changes Over Time**
   - Take new photos of the same mole regularly
   - Use the comparison feature to see changes
   - Add notes about any observations

3. **Stay Organized**
   - Use region overview photos for context
   - Set up monthly reminders
   - Export data for doctor visits

## 📖 How-To Guides

### Adding a New Mole

1. Tap the **"+"** button in the top-right corner
2. Select the **body region** (e.g., "Arm Left")
3. Select the **body side** (e.g., "Upper Arm Front")
4. Optionally add **notes** (e.g., "Near elbow")
5. Tap **"Save"**
6. Tap **"Take Photo"** to capture your first image

### Taking a Photo

1. Open a mole from the list
2. Tap **"Take Photo"**
3. Grant camera permission if prompted
4. **Position your device** to frame the mole
5. Use the **overlay** (if available) to align with previous photos
6. Tap the **shutter button**
7. Photo is automatically saved

**Tips:**
- Use good lighting (natural light is best)
- Keep the same distance each time
- Try to use the same angle
- Avoid shadows on the mole

### Comparing Photos

1. Open a mole with at least 2 photos
2. Tap **"Compare Images"**
3. Select **two photos** to compare (numbered 1 and 2)
4. Tap **"Compare"**
5. Use the **mode toggle** to switch between:
   - Side-by-Side view
   - Overlay view (with transparency slider)
6. **Pinch to zoom** for detailed examination
7. **Swap** photos if needed

### Using Guided Scanning

Perfect for regular check-ups:

1. Tap the **menu (⋯)** in the top-left
2. Select **"Guided Scanning"**
3. Follow the on-screen instructions for each mole
4. Tap **"Take Photo"** when ready
5. Use **"Skip"** for inaccessible moles
6. Use **"Back"** to retake a photo
7. Review **statistics** when complete

### Using Guided Comparison

Systematic review of all moles:

1. Tap the **menu (⋯)** in the top-left
2. Select **"Guided Comparison"**
3. Review each mole's latest photo vs. reference
4. Add **notes** about any changes
5. Use **"Next"** to continue
6. Use **"Skip"** if no comparison needed
7. Review **summary** at the end

### Syncing Between Devices

Transfer data from iPhone to iPad (or vice versa):

**On Source Device:**
1. Tap **menu (⋯)** → **"Sync to Device"**
2. Select **date range** (default: today)
3. Tap **"Preview Export"** to see what will be sent
4. Tap **"Share via AirDrop"**
5. Select your **target device**
6. Wait for transfer to complete

**On Target Device:**
1. **Accept** the AirDrop transfer
2. Nevus opens automatically
3. **Review** the package contents
4. Tap **"Import Data"**
5. Wait for import to complete
6. Review **results**

### Creating Region Overview Photos

Document entire body areas:

1. Scroll to a **body region** in the main list
2. Tap **"Add Overview Photo"** (or the camera icon)
3. Take a **wide shot** of the entire region
4. Add **notes** if desired
5. Photo appears in the region's overview section

### Setting Up Reminders

Get monthly check-up notifications:

1. Tap **menu (⋯)** → **"Settings"** (if available)
2. Enable **"Monthly Reminders"**
3. Grant **notification permission** when prompted
4. Choose your preferred **reminder time**
5. Tap **"Save"**

### Exporting Data

Share with your doctor or backup:

**Export Single Mole:**
1. Open the mole
2. Tap **menu (⋯)** → **"Export Mole"**
3. Choose where to share (Files, Email, etc.)

**Export All Data:**
1. From main screen, tap **menu (⋯)**
2. Select **"Export All Moles"**
3. Choose destination
4. Large ZIP file will be created

**Export Single Image:**
1. Open an image in full screen
2. Tap the **share button**
3. Choose destination

## 🔒 Privacy & Security

### Your Data is Private

- ✅ **Local storage only** - All data stays on your device
- ✅ **No cloud uploads** - Unless you enable iCloud backup
- ✅ **No tracking** - We don't collect usage data

3. **Enable Face ID/Touch ID** for app access
4. **Regular backups** to iCloud or computer
5. **Delete old sync files** after importing

## 🛠️ Troubleshooting

### Camera Issues

**Camera won't open:**
- Check camera permission in Settings → Nevus
- Restart the app
- Restart your device

**Photos are blurry:**
- Clean your camera lens
- Ensure good lighting
- Hold device steady
- Tap to focus before capturing

**Overlay doesn't show:**
- Ensure the mole has at least one previous photo
- Check that reference image is set
- Try restarting the camera

### Sync Issues

**AirDrop not working:**
- Enable Bluetooth and WiFi on both devices
- Ensure devices are unlocked
- Check AirDrop settings (set to "Everyone" or "Contacts Only")
- Move devices closer together

**Import fails:**
- Check available storage space
- Ensure both devices have the latest app version
- Try exporting with a smaller date range
- Restart both devices

**Duplicates after import:**
- This is normal - duplicates are automatically skipped
- The system uses UUIDs to detect duplicates
- Your data is safe and won't be duplicated

### Performance Issues

**App is slow:**
- Close other apps
- Restart the device
- Check available storage (need at least 500MB free)
- Consider deleting old overview photos

**Photos take long to load:**
- This is normal for large collections
- Images are compressed for faster loading
- Thumbnails load first, full images on demand

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### General

**Q: Is Nevus a medical device?**
A: No. Nevus is a tracking tool for personal use. It does not diagnose or treat any medical condition. Always consult a healthcare professional.

**Q: How often should I take photos?**
A: Most dermatologists recommend checking moles monthly. Use the reminder feature to stay on schedule.

**Q: Can I share my data with my doctor?**
A: Yes! Use the export feature to create a ZIP file with all your photos and data. You can email it or bring it on a USB drive.

**Q: Does Nevus work offline?**
A: Yes! All features work offline. Sync requires WiFi/Bluetooth for AirDrop.

### Privacy

**Q: Where is my data stored?**
A: All data is stored locally on your device in the app's secure container. If you enable iCloud, it's also backed up to your personal iCloud account.

**Q: Can anyone else see my photos?**
A: No. Your data is private and only accessible on your device (and your iCloud if enabled). We don't have access to your data.

**Q: Do you collect any data?**
A: No. We don't collect usage data, analytics, or any personal information.

### Features

**Q: How many moles can I track?**
A: There's no limit. The app can handle hundreds of moles with thousands of photos.

**Q: Can I track moles on multiple people?**
A: Currently, the app is designed for single-user tracking. For multiple people, use separate devices or consider future family sharing features.

**Q: What's the difference between a mole photo and an overview photo?**
A: Mole photos are close-ups of individual moles. Overview photos show entire body regions for context.

**Q: Can I delete photos?**
A: Yes. Swipe left on any photo to delete it. Be careful - this cannot be undone!

### Technical

**Q: What iOS version do I need?**
A: iOS 17.6 or later. The app works on iPhone and iPad.

**Q: How much storage does the app use?**
A: It depends on how many photos you take. Each photo is about 1-2 MB. 100 photos = ~100-200 MB.

**Q: Can I use this on my Apple Watch?**
A: Not yet. Apple Watch support is planned for a future version.

**Q: Does this work with HealthKit?**
A: Not yet. HealthKit integration is planned for a future version.

### Medical Resources

**Important**: These are general resources. Always consult your healthcare provider.

- **ABCDE Rule** for mole checking:
  - **A**symmetry - One half doesn't match the other
  - **B**order - Irregular, ragged, or blurred edges
  - **C**olor - Multiple colors or uneven distribution
  - **D**iameter - Larger than 6mm (pencil eraser)
  - **E**volving - Changes in size, shape, or color

- **When to see a doctor:**
  - New moles after age 30
  - Moles that change in size, shape, or color
  - Moles that bleed, itch, or become painful
  - Any concerning changes
