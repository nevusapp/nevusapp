Nevus - User Guide

Welcome to Nevus! 🎯

Nevus is your personal mole tracking companion that helps you monitor skin changes over time. This guide will help you get started and make the most of the app's features.

⚠️ Medical Disclaimer: Nevus is a tracking tool, not a medical device. It is not intended for diagnosis or treatment. Always consult a healthcare professional for medical advice and regular skin examinations.

📱 Quick Start

Getting Started in 3 Steps

  1. Add Your First Mole
  2. Tap the "+" button
  3. Select body region and side
  4. Take a photo with your camera

  5. Add optional notes

  6. Track Changes Over Time

  7. Take new photos of the same mole regularly
  8. Use the comparison feature to see changes

  9. Add notes about any observations

  10. Stay Organized

  11. Use region overview photos for context
  12. Set up monthly reminders
  13. Export data for doctor visits

✨ Core Features

📸 Photo Capture

Take high-quality photos of your moles with: - Camera overlay - Align new photos with previous ones - Reference images - See your last photo while taking a new one - Instant save - Photos are saved immediately

🗂️ Organization

Keep your data organized with: - 12 body regions - Head, neck, arms, torso, back, legs - 26 body sides - Detailed location tracking (front, back, left, right, etc.) - Notes - Add observations for each mole - Timestamps - Automatic date tracking

🔍 Comparison Tools

Compare photos to spot changes: - Side-by-Side - View two photos next to each other - Overlay Mode - Superimpose photos with transparency slider - Zoom & Pan - Examine details closely - Reference Selection - Choose which photo to use as baseline

📊 Overview Images

Capture the big picture: - Region photos - Take overview shots of entire body areas - Context - See where individual moles are located - Notes - Document overall skin condition - Timeline - Track changes across regions

🎯 Guided Workflows

Systematic tracking made easy: - Guided Scanning - Step-by-step photo capture of all moles - Guided Comparison - Systematic review of changes - Progress tracking - See how many moles you've checked - Skip & navigate - Flexible workflow control

🔄 Device Sync

Share data between your devices privately: - AirDrop sync - Transfer data directly between iPhone and iPad - No cloud required - Your data stays on your devices - Delta sync - Only transfer new photos - Duplicate detection - Prevents data duplication

📖 How-To Guides

Adding a New Mole

  1. Tap the "+" button in the top-right corner
  2. Select the body region (e.g., "Arm Left")
  3. Select the body side (e.g., "Upper Arm Front")
  4. Optionally add notes (e.g., "Near elbow")
  5. Tap "Save"
  6. Tap "Take Photo" to capture your first image

Taking a Photo

  1. Open a mole from the list
  2. Tap "Take Photo"
  3. Grant camera permission if prompted
  4. Position your device to frame the mole
  5. Use the overlay (if available) to align with previous photos
  6. Tap the shutter button
  7. Photo is automatically saved

Tips: - Use good lighting (natural light is best) - Keep the same distance each time - Try to use the same angle - Avoid shadows on the mole

Comparing Photos

  1. Open a mole with at least 2 photos
  2. Tap "Compare Images"
  3. Select two photos to compare (numbered 1 and 2)
  4. Tap "Compare"
  5. Use the mode toggle to switch between:
  6. Side-by-Side view
  7. Overlay view (with transparency slider)
  8. Pinch to zoom for detailed examination
  9. Swap photos if needed

Using Guided Scanning

Perfect for regular check-ups:

  1. Tap the menu (⋯) in the top-left
  2. Select "Guided Scanning"
  3. Follow the on-screen instructions for each mole
  4. Tap "Take Photo" when ready
  5. Use "Skip" for inaccessible moles
  6. Use "Back" to retake a photo
  7. Review statistics when complete

Using Guided Comparison

Systematic review of all moles:

  1. Tap the menu (⋯) in the top-left
  2. Select "Guided Comparison"
  3. Review each mole's latest photo vs. reference
  4. Add notes about any changes
  5. Use "Next" to continue
  6. Use "Skip" if no comparison needed
  7. Review summary at the end

Syncing Between Devices

Transfer data from iPhone to iPad (or vice versa):

On Source Device: 1. Tap menu (⋯) → "Sync to Device" 2. Select date range (default: today) 3. Tap "Preview Export" to see what will be sent 4. Tap "Share via AirDrop" 5. Select your target device 6. Wait for transfer to complete

On Target Device: 1. Accept the AirDrop transfer 2. Nevus opens automatically 3. Review the package contents 4. Tap "Import Data" 5. Wait for import to complete 6. Review results

Creating Region Overview Photos

Document entire body areas:

  1. Scroll to a body region in the main list
  2. Tap "Add Overview Photo" (or the camera icon)
  3. Take a wide shot of the entire region
  4. Add notes if desired
  5. Photo appears in the region's overview section

Setting Up Reminders

Get monthly check-up notifications:

  1. Tap menu (⋯) → "Settings" (if available)
  2. Enable "Monthly Reminders"
  3. Grant notification permission when prompted
  4. Choose your preferred reminder time
  5. Tap "Save"

Exporting Data

Share with your doctor or backup:

Export Single Mole: 1. Open the mole 2. Tap menu (⋯) → "Export Mole" 3. Choose where to share (Files, Email, etc.)

Export All Data: 1. From main screen, tap menu (⋯) 2. Select "Export All Moles" 3. Choose destination 4. Large ZIP file will be created

Export Single Image: 1. Open an image in full screen 2. Tap the share button 3. Choose destination

🔒 Privacy & Security

Your Data is Private

  • ✅ Local storage only - All data stays on your device
  • ✅ No cloud uploads - Data only in standard iOS device backup
  • ✅ No tracking - We don't collect usage data
  • ✅ No accounts - No registration required
  • ✅ Encrypted - Data is protected by iOS encryption

iOS Device Backup

Your data is included in standard iOS backups: - iCloud Backup - If you have iCloud Backup enabled on your device - Computer Backup - When you backup via iTunes/Finder - Encrypted - Protected by iOS encryption - Automatic - No app configuration needed - Note: This is standard iOS backup, not app-level sync between devices

AirDrop Sync

When using device-to-device sync: - Transfer is encrypted by iOS - Data never goes through the internet - Direct device-to-device transfer only - No third parties involved

Best Practices

  1. Enable device encryption (iOS does this by default)
  2. Use a strong passcode on your device
  3. Enable Face ID/Touch ID for app access
  4. Regular backups to iCloud or computer
  5. Delete old sync files after importing

🛠️ Troubleshooting

Camera Issues

Camera won't open: - Check camera permission in Settings → Nevus - Restart the app - Restart your device

Photos are blurry: - Clean your camera lens - Ensure good lighting - Hold device steady - Tap to focus before capturing

Overlay doesn't show: - Ensure the mole has at least one previous photo - Check that reference image is set - Try restarting the camera

Sync Issues

AirDrop not working: - Enable Bluetooth and WiFi on both devices - Ensure devices are unlocked - Check AirDrop settings (set to "Everyone" or "Contacts Only") - Move devices closer together

Import fails: - Check available storage space - Ensure both devices have the latest app version - Try exporting with a smaller date range - Restart both devices

Duplicates after import: - This is normal - duplicates are automatically skipped - The system uses UUIDs to detect duplicates - Your data is safe and won't be duplicated

Performance Issues

App is slow: - Close other apps - Restart the device - Check available storage (need at least 500MB free) - Consider deleting old overview photos

Photos take long to load: - This is normal for large collections - Images are compressed for faster loading - Thumbnails load first, full images on demand

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

General

Q: Is Nevus a medical device? A: No. Nevus is a tracking tool for personal use. It does not diagnose or treat any medical condition. Always consult a healthcare professional.

Q: How often should I take photos? A: Most dermatologists recommend checking moles monthly. Use the reminder feature to stay on schedule.

Q: Can I share my data with my doctor? A: Yes! Use the export feature to create a ZIP file with all your photos and data. You can email it or bring it on a USB drive.

Q: Does Nevus work offline? A: Yes! All features work offline. Sync requires WiFi/Bluetooth for AirDrop.

Privacy

Q: Where is my data stored? A: All data is stored locally on your device in the app's secure container. It's included in your standard iOS device backup (iCloud Backup or computer backup).

Q: Can anyone else see my photos? A: No. Your data is private and only accessible on your device (and in your iOS device backup). We don't have access to your data.

Q: Do you collect any data? A: No. We don't collect usage data, analytics, or any personal information.

Features

Q: How many moles can I track? A: There's no limit. The app can handle hundreds of moles with thousands of photos.

Q: Can I track moles on multiple people? A: Currently, the app is designed for single-user tracking. For multiple people, use separate devices or consider future family sharing features.

Q: What's the difference between a mole photo and an overview photo? A: Mole photos are close-ups of individual moles. Overview photos show entire body regions for context.

Q: Can I delete photos? A: Yes. Swipe left on any photo to delete it. Be careful - this cannot be undone!

Technical

Q: What iOS version do I need? A: iOS 17.6 or later. The app works on iPhone and iPad.

Q: How much storage does the app use? A: It depends on how many photos you take. Each photo is about 1-2 MB. 100 photos = ~100-200 MB.

Q: Can I use this on my Apple Watch? A: Not yet. Apple Watch support is planned for a future version.

Q: Does this work with HealthKit? A: Not yet. HealthKit integration is planned for a future version.

Medical Resources

Important: These are general resources. Always consult your healthcare provider.

  • ABCDE Rule for mole checking:
  • Asymmetry - One half doesn't match the other
  • Border - Irregular, ragged, or blurred edges
  • Color - Multiple colors or uneven distribution
  • Diameter - Larger than 6mm (pencil eraser)

  • Evolving - Changes in size, shape, or color

  • When to see a doctor:

  • New moles after age 30
  • Moles that change in size, shape, or color
  • Moles that bleed, itch, or become painful
  • Any concerning changes

🎯 Tips for Best Results

Photography Tips

  1. Consistent lighting - Use the same lighting conditions each time
  2. Same distance - Try to maintain the same distance from the mole
  3. Same angle - Use the overlay feature to match previous angles
  4. Clean lens - Wipe your camera lens before taking photos
  5. Steady hands - Rest your arms on a surface if possible

Organization Tips

  1. Regular schedule - Set monthly reminders and stick to them
  2. Immediate notes - Add notes right after taking photos
  3. Overview photos - Take region overviews every 3-6 months
  4. Guided workflows - Use guided scanning for thorough checks
  5. Export regularly - Keep backups of your data

Comparison Tips

  1. Set reference images - Choose your best baseline photo
  2. Use overlay mode - Best for detecting subtle changes
  3. Zoom in - Don't be afraid to examine details closely
  4. Take notes - Document what you observe
  5. Show your doctor - Bring comparisons to appointments
